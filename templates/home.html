Here are a few facts about bourbon and the requirements to be labeled as bourbon.


  1. For a whiskey to be labeled as a bourbon, it must first be made in America.
  2. Bourbon must also be created from a mash (mixture of fermentable grain) that is at least 51 percent corn. The other 49 percent is usually a mixture of barley, rye, or wheat.
  3. Another requirement is that bourbon must go into the barrel at no more than 125 proof and it cannot be bottled at anything less than 80 proof

For a more detailed list of facts and the history of bourbon, please visit the Bourbon Facts page.
